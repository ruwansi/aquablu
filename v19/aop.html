The Science

The main goal of h2o.TITANIUM is the generation of hydroxyl radicals by means of Advanced Oxidative Photocatalysis, a physicochemical process capable of producing fatal changes in the chemical structure of pollutants, achieving their mineralisation.

The photocatalyst, the h2o.TITANIUM reactor itself, is a broadband semiconductor that is directly excited by the absorption of radiant energy. The photons generated from the UV lamps it contains ensure that at the interface between the excited catalyst (titanium dioxide) and the solution (mainly water and organic compounds), the pollutant degradation reactions take place until their mineralisation is achieved.
